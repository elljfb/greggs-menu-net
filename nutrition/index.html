Understanding Allergen Information

Greggs provides comprehensive allergen information for all menu items. Common allergens in Greggs products include:

  • Gluten (Wheat, Barley, Rye) - Present in most bakes, sandwiches, and pastries
  • Milk - Found in many items including drinks, bakes, and sweet treats
  • Eggs - Used in pastries, mayonnaise, and various fillings
  • Soya - Present in many products including vegan items
  • Mustard - Often in sandwich fillings and sauces
  • Fish - In tuna-based products
  • Sulphites - May be present in various products
  • Celery - Sometimes in savoury fillings

Important: If you have a food allergy or intolerance, always check the allergen information in-store before ordering. Products are prepared in kitchens where allergens are present, so cross-contamination may occur.

Nutritional FAQs

Does Greggs display calorie information?

Yes, calorie information is displayed for all menu items both in-store and on the Greggs website. This helps customers make informed choices about their food.

Are Greggs nutritional values accurate?

Nutritional values are based on standard recipes and typical serving sizes. Actual values may vary slightly depending on preparation method, ingredient suppliers, and portion sizes. Values are provided as a guide.

Can I get allergen information in-store?

Yes, all Greggs shops have allergen information available. Ask staff for an allergen guide, or check the information boards usually displayed near the counter.

Are any Greggs items suitable for specific diets?

Greggs offers options suitable for vegetarians and vegans (clearly marked on the menu). For other dietary requirements like gluten-free, options are limited, so always check current availability with your local shop.

How often is nutritional information updated?

When recipes change or new items are introduced, nutritional information is updated accordingly. For the most current information, always check the official Greggs website or in-store materials.

Does Greggs offer nutritional advice?

Greggs provides nutritional information to help you make informed choices, but does not offer personalised nutritional advice. For dietary guidance, consult a registered dietitian or nutritionist.
